Transmission Control Protocol & User Datagram Protocol


TCP

  • Established connection that maintains until application programs at each end have finished exchanging messages.
  • Data transferred remains intact guaranteed.
  • Erroneous packets are retransmitted upon check.
  • Suited for reliability, at the expense of time.

UDP

  • Packets sent to recipient regardless of acquiring frequency.
  • Packets not received are lost for transmission.
  • Does not protect against datagram duplication; can receive multiple copies of same UDP datagram.
  • Suited for speed and efficiency, and answering queries from many clients.
